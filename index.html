LAUNCH OF USAT 2026 ▼
Issued by: Francisco De Los Reyes, ASA Director • Thru: Carlos De La Pena, Admission Director
  1. The USAT will be administered starting from the release of this memorandum and will run for 2 months.
  2. USAT registrations and applications will ONLY run through the ONLINE PORTAL and will NOT accommodate walk-in applicants.
  3. The 2 months of USAT will be allotted for ADMISSION, while the last one month will be the window for SCHOLARSHIP application.
  4. USAT will take place through face-to-face examination with different branches throughout the international southern campus.
  5. This memorandum contains enclosures: the admission process, USAT examination, Admission Competitiveness Index (ACI), and admission status and appeals.
Enclosure 1: Admission Process ▼

1. S-Registration

The admission starts with registration. Applicants shall create their own account through the official admission website. Once registration is finished, an email confirmation containing the S-REGISTER NUMBER (format: 26-#####) will be sent to the applicant.

2. S-Application

Once registered, applicants log in using their S-Register Number and custom password to complete the application. A payment of PHP 1,500 shall be settled, or the applicant may apply for a fee waiver.

3. S-Application Approval

Submitted applications undergo approval. Once approved, the applicant receives the Application ID (AID#####). Applications may be returned for revisions or rejected due to incomplete payment.

4. USAT Examination

Approved applicants choose their examination date and testing center. The slot is confirmed with finality once selected. Applicants are emailed their examination permit once the admission office confirms.

5. For Releasing of Results

After examination, the university sets the application status to "For Releasing of Results." If the applicant did not take the exam, the status is automatically voided.

Purpose

The USAT is a standardized assessment evaluating academic preparedness and competitiveness for admission across basic education, senior high school, and undergraduate programs.

Pointing System

Each correct answer = 1 point. Wrong answers use a Progressive Penalty System: first 10 wrong = –0.25 each; next 10 wrong = –0.50 each; all subsequent wrong = –1.00 each. Unanswered = 0. Scores released as Normalized Performance Percentage (NPP).
Enclosure 3: Admission Competitiveness Index (ACI) ▼

The ACI is a ranked admission metric. The computation method is confidential. Admission is strictly rank-based per program; meeting the cut-off does not guarantee admission.

Enclosure 4: Admission Results and Appeals ▼

Official results are released two (2) months after the examination date. Possible statuses:

  • Submitted applications cannot be edited unless the status is "For Revisions."
  • Application ID (AID) is issued only upon approval of application.
  • Email address must be valid for confirmation and notifications.
